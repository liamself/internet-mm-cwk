Local Attractions

Norwich and Norfolk have many interesting things to do.

Norwich

Culture

Norwich is a very historical city, filled with culture. Norwich Castle is a Medieval Castle situated on a hill in the heart of the city. Open to guests year-round, the castle is an interesting way to explore history, as well as providing great views over the city. Seasonal events and exhibitions also visit the castle, meaning there is always something new to enjoy every visit.

The Sainsbury Centre for Visual Arts, found at the University of East Anglia, is home to many exciting pieces of art, as well as visiting exhibitions. Enjoy a visit to the gallery, a stroll around the sculpture park, and relax afterwards at the Modern Life Cafe. Film fans may also recognise the building from many Marvel films.

Norwich is also home to many theatres. Catch a show at the Theatre Royal, Playhouse, or Puppet Theatre. Many local bars also offer live music in the evenings.

Whilst here, why not complete the 'Norwich 12', by visiting 12 of the most historically interesting buildings in Norwich.

Business

Norwich is the perfect place to find new business opportunities. The Norwich Research Park is located just minutes from our hotel. We also have conference facilities, making this the optimal place to hold conferences.

Shopping

Norwich is the perfect place to indulge in some retail therapy. With 2 large shopping centres, it is easy to spend hours browsing some of the most popular high street stores. Furthermore, with the city centre easily accessible from the hotel by bus or car, you don't need to worry about carrying your bags too far!

For shoppers seeking something a little more unique, Norwich is also home to many independent shops. In the Norwich Lanes you can find a number of small independent retailers selling all kinds of interesting products. Norwich is also home to Jarrolds, an independent department store which stocks many Norfolk-local products as well as national and international brands.

Norfolk

Whilst staying with us, why not go further afield and visit more of Norfolk!

Norfolk is full of interesting wildlife. A visit to the broads is a chance to reconnect with nature, escape from city life, and enjoy the peace and quiet of the beautiful surroundings.

Not interested in nature? Norfolk is also full of beautiful little villages which are perfect to explore for an afternoon, or stop in for a drink in a local pub.

Norfolk is also home to some award-winning beaches. Just a short drive, bus or train journey from the hotel you can find Sheringham, where you can enjoy ice cream on the beach and Cromer, famous for its crabs. Alternatively, you can head to Great Yarmouth for a seaside experience complete with arcades.
